Energy.gov

Provide a faster, more organized user experience to direct DOE audiences to the critical information they seek.

Problem

Energy.gov had lost sight of who it was serving. Since the last rebuild of their website, many owners and admins had begun making assumptions on where and how to add content. It became increasingly difficult for the public to find the answers to clean energy, climate change and other top searches sought after on the website. The redesign needed to include a new content strategy, new information architecture, modern design and meet 508 compliance under the 21st Century Idea for all government applications.

I was one of two UX Designers on the website. We were tasked with running working sessions with the client to develop a solid sitemap. From there we began building out wireframes based on the content strategy established through the working sessions of developing the sitemap. Prototypes were then developed and I also worked on the UI with drupal developers.

Solution

Energy.gov will provide the public with an easy to use website that has a fresh modern brand. The new design that includes easy to use navigation, great search and impactful photography will make the experience on energy.gov and enjoyable one.
